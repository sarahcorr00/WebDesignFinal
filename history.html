The Gaelic Athletic Association (GAA) was founded in 1884, It was created to revitalise Irish national pastimes such as Gaelic football and hurling. The regrowth of Irish sport led to a broader sense of Irish nationalism amongst the Irish people.

There was seven founding members of the GAA with Michael Cusack being the driving force of its establishment. Cusack was an Irish teacher from County Clare. Michael Cusack was active in the fight of the Gaelic revival he wanted to preserve the Irish language and customs.

On November 1 1884, Cusack met with six other men in Hayes Hotel in Thurles, County Tipperary. Together they founded the Gaelic Athletic Association for the Cultivation and Preservation of National Pastimes a title later shortened to the Gaelic Athletic Association (GAA).s
